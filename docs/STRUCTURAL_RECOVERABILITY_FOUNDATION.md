STRUCTURAL RECOVERABILITY FOUNDATION

Core Idea

A structural collapse does not necessarily imply structural destruction.

Two structures may become indistinguishable under a given observer family while remaining separable under a richer projection space.

Therefore:

collapse != annihilation

The key question becomes:

Can the latent structure be recovered
from additional observers?

This defines:

STRUCTURAL RECOVERABILITY


---

1. Structural Space

Let:

S

be the latent structural space.

A structure is:

s ∈ S

Observers do not access s directly.

They only access projections.


---

2. Projection Operators

A projection operator is:

π : S → O

where:

O

is the observable space.

Different observers induce different projections.

Define an observer family:

P = {π₁, π₂, ..., πₙ}


---

3. Projection-Induced Equivalence

Two structures become indistinguishable under a projection family when:

∀π ∈ P:

π(s₁) = π(s₂)

Define:

s₁ ~P s₂

This is not necessarily true equivalence.

It is:

projection-relative equivalence


---

4. Structural Collapse

A structural collapse occurs when:

distinct latent structures
map to identical observable projections

Formally:

s₁ ≠ s₂
but
s₁ ~P s₂

This produces:

STRUCTURAL INDISTINGUISHABILITY

under observer family P.


---

5. Recoverability

Introduce an expanded observer family:

P' ⊃ P

If there exists at least one observer:

π* ∈ P'

such that:

π*(s₁) ≠ π*(s₂)

then the collapse is recoverable.

Define recoverability:

Recoverable(P, s₁, s₂)
iff
∃π ∈ P':
π(s₁) ≠ π(s₂)


---

6. Irrecoverable Collapse

A collapse becomes irrecoverable when:

∀ admissible observer families P*:

s₁ ~P* s₂

meaning:

no projection expansion
can separate the structures

This defines:

GLOBAL STRUCTURAL COLLAPSE


---

7. Recoverability Operator

Define:

R(P, s₁, s₂)

as the minimal observer expansion required to separate two collapsed structures.

Conceptually:

R = minimal additional observability
required for recovery


---

8. Recoverability Score

Define:

ρ(s₁, s₂)

as a normalized recoverability score.

Example interpretation:

ρ = 1
fully recoverable

ρ = 0
irrecoverable

Intermediate values represent partial recoverability.


---

9. Observer Geometry

Observer families induce a geometry over projection space.

Some observers are close:

highly redundant projections

Others are distant:

structurally orthogonal projections

Define observer distance:

D(πᵢ, πⱼ)

Recovery becomes easier when observer diversity increases.


---

10. Projection Persistence

Some structures remain invariant across many observer families.

Define persistence:

Persistence(s)
=
stability across observer families

High persistence implies:

observer-independent structure

Low persistence implies:

observer-fragile structure


---

11. Local vs Global Collapse

Local Collapse

collapse under limited observer family

Recoverable.


---

Global Collapse

collapse under all admissible observer families

Irrecoverable.


---

12. Core Boundary

This framework does NOT claim:

semantic truth
ontology
physical identity
meaning equivalence

It only measures:

projection-relative recoverability

Therefore:

measurement != inference != decision


---

13. Main Insight

The central insight is:

observed indistinguishability
does not imply
latent equivalence

because:

projection collapse
may hide recoverable structure

The problem is therefore not merely:

What is observed?

but:

What remains recoverable
under expanded observability?
